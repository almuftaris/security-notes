# Handala Hack: St. Joseph County, Indiana Breach Claim
**Date:** April 1, 2026
**Reported:** April 2, 2026
**Source:** Google Threat Intelligence Group (GTIG) Report 26-10017022
**Related notes:** Handala Hack: Operations March 24–30, 2026

---

## Summary

On April 1, 2026, Handala Hack claimed a comprehensive breach of the centralized IT infrastructure of **St. Joseph County, Indiana**. The group stated the operation followed months of reconnaissance and deliberate planning. GTIG independently observed the claims and assessed the described tactics as consistent with Handala's documented behavior. The group framed the attack as a geopolitical warning, stating its responses to actions against the "Axis of Resistance" will be "crushing and unpredictable."

---

## Claimed Impact

- **2+ TB** of sensitive records exfiltrated
- **12 TB** of data destroyed from primary servers, described as unrecoverable
- Affected entities allegedly include the St. Joseph County Prosecutor's Office, local police departments, and regional medical centers
- Over 2,000 confidential documents related to local officials and municipal agencies released publicly
- A broad fax notification campaign sent attack details to tens of thousands of organizations across the US

---

## Significance

Two things make this claim worth tracking beyond typical hacktivist noise:

**Targeting logic.** St. Joseph County is not a symbolic or defense-adjacent target — it's a mid-sized county government in Indiana with no obvious connection to US-Iran policy. GTIG notes that the FBI domain seizures on March 19 directly triggered Handala's broadening of its targeting calculus to include US civilian and government infrastructure at the local level. The county-level targeting pattern is consistent with the North Country POS and Montana grocery store claims from the week prior — Handala appears to be deliberately targeting unglamorous, under-defended civilian infrastructure to generate fear disproportionate to actual strategic impact.

**The fax campaign.** Sending attack details via fax to tens of thousands of US organizations is an unusual amplification method. It serves no technical purpose — it is purely psychological. The intent is to make defenders feel watched and to force organizations with no connection to the breach to wonder whether they are next. This kind of mass psychological notification is an influence operation layered on top of the technical one.

---

## GTIG Analyst Assessment

GTIG confirmed independent observation of Handala's claims and assessed the described tactics — data exfiltration combined with destructive deletion — as consistent with the group's documented behavior. The report noted the attack fits within a pattern of Handala expanding US targeting following the March 19 DOJ domain seizures, which the group has consistently framed as acts of aggression requiring escalating retaliation.

GTIG reaffirmed attribution: Handala Hack (UNC5203 / overlaps with UNC4074) remains assessed as an MOIS-linked persona. The domain seizures targeting Handala were part of a broader US government enforcement action against MOIS-attributed influence infrastructure, which also included Homeland Justice and Karmabelow80 under the same UNC4074 cluster.

---

## Notes on Credibility

The GTIG report does not assign an explicit credibility rating to the St. Joseph County claim beyond confirming the tactics are "consistent" with Handala's documented behavior. The 12 TB destruction figure and the "months-long reconnaissance" framing are unverified by third parties. Handala has a documented pattern of inflating damage claims — the Stryker 200,000-device figure versus BleepingComputer's confirmed ~80,000, and the Kash Patel "FBI internal systems" claim versus the actual personal Gmail breach. The release of 2,000 documents as proof of concept is the strongest substantiating evidence available publicly.
